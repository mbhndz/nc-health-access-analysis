\# NC County Health Burden and Care Access Analysis



This is a small exploratory analysis of county-level health burden and physician access in North Carolina using public health data.



The project is mainly intended to practice and demonstrate basic data cleaning, exploratory analysis, and written interpretation with a real dataset.



\## Analysis



The notebook:



1\. Loads and inspects the county health data.

2\. Cleans selected variables for analysis.

3\. Compares counties by health burden and physician access.

4\. Identifies counties with health burden and physician access both above median levels.

5\. Compares this group with statewide county medians on a few related variables.



\## Main Observations



The counties identified by this simple definition differ from the statewide median in the expected directions for health burden and physician access, since these variables were used to define the group.



They also show higher childhood poverty and lower household broadband access. The uninsured rate is close to the median over all counties, suggesting that it does not strongly distinguish this group in this comparison.



These observations are descriptive. They are not meant to establish causes or make policy claims.
